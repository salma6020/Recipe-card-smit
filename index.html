CHAPLI KABABS

           

Ingredients

  • 1kg minced beef
  • 1 large onion chopped
  • 2 tomatoes chopped
  • 6 green chilies chopped
  • 2 egg (optional for extra binding)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds crushed
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for fry
  • besan 2 tablespoon
  • Shan Chapli kabab masala

Methods:

1. Prepare the Mixture

In a large mixing bowl combine the minced beef, chopped onions tomatoes (without pulp), green chilies
coriander, mint leaves, and all the spices (crushed coriander, cumin seeds, red chili powder, black pepper
and salt). Add the ginger-garlic paste and gram flour. Mix well.

2. Rest the Mixture

the mixture to rest for 30 minutes. This helps the flavors combine and firms up the mixture. if the mixture feels too wet add a little more flour for binding.

3. Shape the Mixture

Take a small portion of the mixture and flatten it into a patty shape. Chapli Kababs are larger and thinner in
Use a smaller amount of mince beef per kabab. One method is to form the kabab on top of a grease paper-lined plate,
using damp hands to press down and shape the kabab into a circle. This method will allow you good amount of control over
the size, roundness and thickness of the kabab. your hands are damp as you shape the kababs will help tremendously.

4. Fry the Kababs

Heat ghee or oil in a frying pan over medium heat. place the kababs in the hot oil and fry until golden brown on one side (about 3-4 minutes). Flip and cook the other side until crispy and cooked through. Use a slotted spoon to remove and place the kababs on a paper towel to absorb excess oil.

5.Serving

Serve hot Chapli kababs with naan, raita and a fresh salad.
